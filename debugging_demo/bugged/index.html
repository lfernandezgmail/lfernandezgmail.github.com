Find the Bugs (Code With Bugs)

When first starting to use CSS students often have trouble getting their CSS to render. Bugs can happen because of many reasons:

Because of syntactical mistakes in the declaration block.

The most common syntactical mistakes are using a parenthesis instead of a curly brace. Or using a colon when a semi-colon is needed. Or vice versa. Or leaving off a semi-colon entirely. Or forgetting to close a rule-set with a closing brace. This last mistake will often corrupt css that is below it so it's especially costly

Because of syntactical mistakes in a selector.

The most common syntactical mistakes in selectors are forgetting to put a "." in front of a class or a "#" in front of an id. Or inadvertently putting a "." or a "#" in the classname in the html.

Because the path in the link tag is wrong.

If nothing is styling most likely it's because your haven't coded the path correctly in your link tag.
